## The task was “reply to one email”

The actual experience was opening the inbox, seeing twelve other things, rereading one thread, checking the calendar, losing the original reason for being there, and feeling the day get noisier.

That is the gap Lifecoach is designed for: not the idealised productivity moment, but the hour where you have already spent your energy deciding where to start.

## What helped was not a better folder system

The helpful move was sorting the pile into four plain buckets: answer now, schedule, save for later, and archive. Then one draft. Not the perfect draft. Just enough words that editing became easier than starting.

AI is useful here when it behaves less like a guru and more like a calm external executive function: reflect the pile, choose one door, and keep the next action small.

## The goal is momentum without shame

ADHD support copy often turns patronising fast. Lifecoach should not sound like a laminated office poster. It should sound like someone sitting beside you saying: this is a lot, and the first move can be smaller than your brain is making it.
