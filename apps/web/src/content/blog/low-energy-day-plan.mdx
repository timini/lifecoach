## Some days the plan has to start with capacity

A normal productivity template asks what you want to accomplish. On a low-energy day, a better first question is: what is the kindest useful thing that still counts?

That might be eating something before answering messages. It might be sending one sentence instead of the full explanation. It might be moving an appointment rather than silently missing it.

## The smallest useful day still deserves structure

Lifecoach is built to help with that version of planning. It can hold the whole messy list while you choose one necessary task, one body-support task, and one permission to stop.

This is not about pretending a chat fixes depression. It is about reducing the executive-function tax at the exact moment daily admin gets heavy.

## A good assistant should not make you perform wellness

The tone matters. People do not need forced brightness when they are depleted. They need a next step that respects the reality of the day.
